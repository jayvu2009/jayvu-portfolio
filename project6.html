MOTION GRAPHICS

PARIS IN THE RAIN

After Effects Premiere Pro Illustrator

Paris in the Rain is a lyric-based motion graphics piece inspired by Lauv’s song of the same name. It combines hand-drawn illustration, animated typography, musical timing, and cinematic transitions to translate the song’s warmth and emotion into a visual story.

WATCH VIDEO

OVERVIEW

This project explores how lyric animation can turn music into an emotional visual experience. Inspired by the romantic atmosphere of “Paris in the Rain,” the piece pairs illustrated scenes with expressive typography and carefully paced transitions. Each visual choice supports the lyrics, helping the story move naturally between intimate moments, changing moods, and the rhythm of the song.

GOALS

Visual Storytelling

Build a clear emotional journey through connected illustrated scenes.

Typography Animation

Use lyric movement and emphasis to strengthen meaning and mood.

Music Synchronization

Align motion, transitions, and pacing with the song’s rhythm.

CONCEPT

The creative direction imagines Paris as a soft, hand-drawn world shaped by rain, romance, and memory. Gentle colours and illustrated environments create warmth, while emotional pacing allows quieter moments to breathe before moving into more energetic lyric sequences. Rain becomes both an atmospheric detail and a visual thread connecting the scenes.

TARGET AUDIENCE

The project is intended for music and motion-design audiences, especially viewers who enjoy lyric videos, illustrated storytelling, romantic visual narratives, and short-form animation. The accessible combination of music, words, and imagery allows the piece to connect with both design-focused viewers and general audiences.

MOTION DESIGN PROCESS

I began by analysing the lyrics and identifying changes in tone, rhythm, and imagery. Those moments shaped the storyboard and helped establish the sequence of scenes. I then developed the illustrations, animated the typography and visual elements, refined the timing against the music, and polished transitions and effects so the full piece would feel cohesive.

DEVELOPMENT

Adobe Illustrator was used to build the hand-drawn visual assets and prepare layered artwork for animation. Adobe After Effects brought the scenes to life through typography, compositing, effects, and camera movement. Adobe Premiere Pro supported the final edit, audio alignment, and export workflow.

After Effects Premiere Pro Illustrator

MOTION TECHNIQUES

Kinetic Typography

Lyrics change in scale, position, and pace to reflect vocal emphasis and emotional tone.

Scene Transitions

Connected transitions carry the viewer smoothly between locations, ideas, and moods.

Illustration Animation

Layered artwork uses subtle movement to make each hand-drawn scene feel alive.

Camera Movement

Pans, pushes, and reframing guide attention and add cinematic depth.

Rain Effects

Animated rain builds atmosphere and creates continuity throughout the visual story.

Audio Synchronization

Key movements and transitions are timed to lyrics, beats, and changes in the music.

FINAL VIDEO

REFLECTION

This project showed me how important storyboard planning is before animation begins. Timing movement to music took patience, but it helped me become more comfortable in After Effects and more intentional with every transition. The biggest challenge was balancing illustration and typography so neither competed for attention. By refining that balance, I learned how motion can carry a story and make the emotion of a song feel more immediate.
