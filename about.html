MY JOURNEY

I once thought I would build a life around people

In high school, I was Vice President of two clubs: F-Event and IDo. One organized every major event in the school. The other organized charity concerts and fundraising campaigns, traveling to remote areas to support underprivileged communities. I loved it. Planning. Coordinating. Listening. Watching people come together because of something we created.

For a long time, I thought this would be my path.

But passions sometimes evolves

What I loved wasn’t just organizing events, it was designing experiences. The way lighting changed a stage atmosphere, the way visuals shaped emotions, the way a small detail could make someone feel seen. I initially considered studying marketing. But when I moved to Canada in December 2024, the intake wasn’t until the following September. I couldn’t afford to wait. So I enrolled in Graphic Design instead and thinking it would simply “fill the gap.”

It didn’t fill a gap. It opened a new door for me.

The first website I coded changed everything

It started as a simple assignment which is rebuilding a template that already existed. But I got hooked fast.

I loved the feeling of figuring out how each element worked, and even more, the moment when a bug finally made sense after hours of being stuck. That tiny “it works!” moment was addictive.

That’s when I knew.

I didn’t just want to organize experiences, I wanted to build them. Not for a single night but for everyday screens. Not just emotionally but functionally.

I design with empathy and precision.

I care about the details: spacing, flow, interaction, hierarchy. The invisible decisions that quietly shape how someone feels.

I’m now working toward my first role as a UX/UI Designer or Front-end Developer, where I can keep learning, building, and creating experiences that truly connect.
