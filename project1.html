DESIGN

NONNA’S THREAD

Illustrator Procreate InDesign

Nonna’s Thread is a branding project focused on creating a handmade pasta identity using Canadian wheat and reusable fabric packaging to reflect sustainability and heritage.

OVERVIEW

Nonna’s Thread is a rebranding project for an artisan pasta brand rooted in heritage and sustainability, combining Italian culinary tradition with Canadian-grown wheat through a warm, character-driven visual identity and reusable fabric packaging.

GOALS

The goal of Nonna’s Thread was to modernize a heritage-driven pasta brand while preserving its sense of tradition, warmth, and sustainability through a cohesive visual identity and reusable packaging system.

DESIGN PROCESS

MOOD BOARD

LOGO SKETCHES

Research

Research focused on heritage pasta brands, sustainable packaging systems, and the emotional connection between food and family tradition. Insights from market positioning and visual analysis guided the balance between authenticity, warmth, and contemporary branding.

Adobe Illustrator

The logo was designed as a flowing thread that subtly resembles a strand of pasta, symbolizing the connection between heritage and the present. The continuous line represents tradition passed down through generations, while the soft curves reflect warmth, family, and handmade authenticity.

ProCreate

The mascot was illustrated in Procreate by personifying pasta strands into playful characters to bring warmth and personality to the reusable fabric bag. Instead of a minimal or overly plain surface, the illustrated elements add charm and visual interest, making the packaging feel more engaging and memorable.

InDesign

The final packaging layout was assembled in InDesign to ensure structured composition, consistent typography, and proper print setup. Using InDesign allowed for precise alignment, margin control, and preparation of a clean, presentation-ready final document.

FINAL OUTCOME

REFLECTION

This project strengthened my understanding that visual identity must communicate meaning beyond aesthetics. I learned that every design decision, from a single line in the logo to the illustrated mascot, should clearly reflect the brand’s heritage and values. In future branding projects, I aim to approach design with stronger strategic intention and deeper research from the start.
